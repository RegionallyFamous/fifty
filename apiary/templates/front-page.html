Batch log · No. 147

Pulled from three hives in the back meadow, last Tuesday.

The bees work a twelve-acre patch of clover, buckwheat, and late-summer goldenrod. We pull frames when the comb is capped, strain once through nothing finer than a kitchen sieve, and let gravity do the rest. The colour of each batch is whatever the meadow decides that month.
